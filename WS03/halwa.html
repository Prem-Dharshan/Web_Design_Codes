Speciality of Halwa

  • Halwa is a type of confectionery originating from Persia and widely spread throughout the Middle East as well as South Asia.
  • It is a sweet dish that is made from flour, butter, liquid oil, saffron, rosewater, milk, cocoa powder, and sweetened with sugar.
  • Halwa is a dense confectionery that is sweetened with sugar or honey and has a variety of textures depending on the recipe. For example, semolina-based halwa's texture can be like a very buttery, moist clumpy couscous to something gelatinous and translucent, while sesame-based halwa is drier and more crumbly.
